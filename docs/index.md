# About
I'm a Nuclear Engineering PhD candidate and DOE UNLP Fellow at NC State. I spend most of my time finding ways to improve numerical methods for large-scale physics simulations. My research focuses on computational methods for radiation transport and high-energy density physics; think nuclear reactors and the extreme conditions inside stars. <br> <br>
In practice, that means writing a lot of code, doing a lot of math, and solving problems that actually matter at scale. My recent work has focused on reducing simulation memory usage, an important consideration when you're running jobs on some of the world's largest supercomputers. <br> <br>
Before starting my PhD, I was a math and engineering student at Western Carolina University, and that combination still defines how I think: find the elegant solution, then make it work in the real world. <br><br>
